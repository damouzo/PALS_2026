# `data_demo/` — Biological Test Dataset for the scMultiome-GRN Pipeline

This directory is the **single source of truth** for everything related to
the test data of the `scMultiome-GRN` Nextflow pipeline. It replaces the
former `test_data/` folder at the repository root and is structured so that
all raw, processed and synthetic test assets live under one directory tree.

The deliverable is a **600-nucleus mouse-microglia dataset** with three
biologically meaningful CellTypes that capture the Homeostatic → DAM
transition, ATAC peaks restricted to chromosomes 7 (Apoe), 15 (Slc1a3) and
17 (Trem2), barcode-synchronized with the original fragment file, and
emitted as both `.h5ad` and Seurat `.rds`.

---

## 1. Biological question

> How does the in silico virtual knock-out of the master neuroinflammatory
> regulators **Apoe** (chr7) and **Trem2** (chr17) disrupt the Gene
> Regulatory Network (GRN) of mouse microglia, and arrest the dynamic
> cell-fate trajectory vectors driving cells from a **Homeostatic** state
> into the **Disease-Associated Microglia (DAM)** active phenotype?

The 10x Genomics AppNote (*Single Cell and Spatial Multiomics Identifies
Alzheimer's Disease Markers*, LIT000170) confirmed that the *Slc1a3* distal
open-chromatin region is the canonical microglia AD signal, and that
**Egr1 / Klf4** are the upstream regulators of that program. Our ATAC
pruning keeps chr7+chr15+chr17 precisely because those three loci
together reproduce the AppNote's *microglia AD regulatory axis* in a
tractable laptop-friendly dataset.

## 3. Cohort design: 3 microglia CellTypes (official 10x clusters)

`subsample_dataset.py` uses the **OFFICIAL** Cell Ranger ARC 2.0.1
`graphclust` cluster labels shipped inside `analysis.tar.gz`. These labels
were generated by 10x with Seurat v4 on the full 33,459-cell dataset and
are the ground truth for cell-type annotation.

| CellType | Biological state | Genotype / age | GEX cluster(s) | Available microglia |
|----------|------------------|----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| `Microglia_Homeostatic` | Homeostatic (surveying) | WT 13.2 m | 21 | 211 |
| `Microglia_EarlyDAM`    | Early DAM (Trem2-dependent program on) | TgCRND8 5.7 m | 21, 22 | 263 |
| `Microglia_LateDAM`     | Late DAM (Lipid-handling / Apoe program) | TgCRND8 17.9 m | 22 | 186 |

These three CellTypes are the minimal design that supports the
Homeostatic → Early-DAM → Late-DAM trajectory described in the AppNote.

The cluster IDs are determined empirically by inspecting
`analysis/clustering/gex/graphclust/differential_expression.csv`:
**GEX cluster 21** is the cluster where 11 of 12 canonical microglia
markers (*Hexb, C1qa, C1qb, C1qc, Cx3cr1, Csf1r, Trem2, Tmem119, Cd68,
P2ry13, Aif1*) are maximally expressed. **GEX cluster 22** is enriched for
*P2ry12* specifically and corresponds to the DAM-like state.

## 4. Sample-to-barcode-suffix mapping

| Barcode suffix | Sample          | n_cells | Role                  |
|----------------|-----------------|---------|-----------------------|
| `-3`, `-4`     | AD_17p9_rep4/5  | 3,650   | Late-DAM cohort       |
| `-7`, `-8`     | AD_5p7_rep2/6   | 1,305   | Early-DAM cohort      |
| `-9`, `-10`    | WT_13p2_rep2/5  | 13,860  | Homeostatic cohort    |
| `-5`, `-6`     | AD_2p5_rep2/3   | 3,210   | (excluded — pre-symptomatic) |
| `-11` to `-14` | WT_2p5 / WT_5p7 | 11,434  | (excluded — pre-symptomatic) |

Verified empirically against `summary.csv` and `analysis.tar.gz`. The
wild-type cohort timepoints are **2.5, 5.7, 13.2 months** (not 13.4 — the
AppNote uses 13.2 m explicitly). TgCRND8 timepoints are **2.5, 5.7, 17.9
months**. 13.2 m is the only post-symptomatic WT timepoint; 17.9 m is the
only post-symptomatic TgCRND8 timepoint.

## 5. Genomic pruning: chr7 + chr15 + chr17

| Chromosome | Why kept | Key gene(s) |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| **chr7**   | The *Apoe* locus (lipid-handling regulator in DAM) | *Apoe* |
| **chr15**  | The *Slc1a3* locus with the differentially accessible distal peak highlighted in the 10x AppNote | *Slc1a3*, *Egr1*, *Klf4* (motif enrichment) |
| **chr17**  | The *Trem2* locus (canonical microglia receptor) | *Trem2*, *Tyrobp* |

Pruning to these three chromosomes reduces the ATAC fragment file from
~6.5 GB to ~500 MB while keeping every regulatory locus that the AppNote
identified as biologically interesting in microglia. Cicero co-accessibility
computations complete in < 30 s on a laptop.

## 6. Subsample design and rationale

The pipeline must execute end-to-end in under 2 minutes on a dual-core
laptop (see `AGENTS.md`). The aggregated multiome is 6.5 GB; running
Cicero on the whole brain is intractable in a demo setting. We
downsample along **three orthogonal axes**:

1. **Cellular axis** — use the OFFICIAL 10x graphclust cluster labels to
   extract microglia (GEX cluster 21) and DAM-like (GEX cluster 22)
   cells. 200 cells per CellType. Total: 600 microglia nuclei.
2. **Genomic axis** — keep only ATAC peaks on chromosomes 7, 15 and 17.
3. **Determinism** — fixed `seed=23` makes the output byte-identical
   across runs.

### Why 200 + 200 + 200 (600 total)?

The 10x AppNote only published **211 homeostatic WT_13p2 microglia**,
**263 AD_5p7 microglia** and **186 AD_17p9 DAM-like microglia** — these
are the hard biological limits. CellOracle's KNN-imputation step uses
`k = 0.025 * n_cells`, so 600 cells give k=15 neighbors — sufficient for
stable GRN inference in the published CellOracle tutorials.

If you want to scale up, pass `--cells-per-cohort 300` and the script
will downsize automatically to whatever the cohort can supply. To use a
fully custom design, pass `--cohorts /path/to/your_cohorts.yaml` (see
Section 8).

## 7. Quickstart

### 7.1 Hardware requirements

| Resource  | Minimum                     | Recommended                |
|-----------|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Disk      | 15 GB free                   | 30 GB free                 |
| RAM       | 4 GB                         | 8 GB                       |
| CPU       | 2 cores                      | 4 cores                    |
| Network   | 50 Mbps (≈ 30 min for 6.5 GB) | 200 Mbps                  |

### 7.2 Step 1 — Download the raw assets

```bash
cd data_demo
bash download_data.sh            # idempotent; resume-safe
# or, to re-download everything:
bash download_data.sh --force
```

The downloader prints a per-file progress banner, total elapsed time,
and a final validation pass. It uses `curl` if available, falling back
to `wget` automatically.

### 7.3 Step 2 — Subsample to a microglia-focused dataset

```bash
# Run inside the Python GRN container (scanpy/anndata are preinstalled):
docker login ghcr.io                                    # one-time
docker run --rm \
    -u $(id -u):$(id -g) \
    -e HOME=/tmp \
    -e MPLCONFIGDIR=/tmp/matplotlib \
    -e NUMBA_CACHE_DIR=/tmp/numba_cache \
    -e XDG_CACHE_HOME=/tmp/xdg \
    -v $(pwd)/data_demo:/workspace/data_demo \
    -w /workspace \
    ghcr.io/damouzo/pals-python-grn:1.0.0 \
    python /workspace/data_demo/subsample_dataset.py

# Or, with a local venv:
python -m venv .venv && source .venv/bin/activate
pip install numpy pandas scipy scanpy anndata h5py
python data_demo/subsample_dataset.py

# Or, with plain pip on a developer laptop:
pip install scanpy anndata pandas numpy scipy h5py
python data_demo/subsample_dataset.py --no-rds   # skip Seurat conversion

# Scale up or down uniformly:
python data_demo/subsample_dataset.py --cells-per-cohort 300
```

> **Memory budget**: the full 10x multiome matrix (33k cells x 99k features,
> sparse CSR) peaks at ~1.8 GB RSS. On a laptop with < 4 GB free RAM this
> step will be OOM-killed. In that case, run the subsample on a real HPC
> node (Apocrita, SLURM) and copy the produced `.rds` back to the laptop:
>
> ```bash
> # On the HPC, after data_demo/download_data.sh has populated data_demo/raw/
> sbatch data_demo/subsample_apocrita.sh        # see script for resource caps
> scp ${HOSTNAME}:data_demo/processed/microglia_dam_demo.rds \
>     data_demo/processed/
> ```

# Use a fully custom design:
python data_demo/subsample_dataset.py --cohorts my_cohorts.yaml
```

Expected runtime: **3–10 minutes** on a laptop (the ATAC fragment
filtering step is the bottleneck; `tabix` is used automatically if
present on `PATH`).

### 7.4 Step 3 — Plug into the Nextflow pipeline

```bash
nextflow run main.nf \
    --input  data_demo/processed/microglia_dam_demo.rds \
    --genome mm10 \
    --ko_targets Apoe,Trem2 \
    -profile docker
```

The pipeline will treat this `.rds` exactly as it did the synthetic
`test_data/mini_seurat.rds`; the only difference is that the data are
**biologically meaningful** and the perturbations target the correct
mouse regulators.

## 8. Custom cohort spec (`--cohorts`)

You can pass a YAML or JSON file to `--cohorts` to override the default
3-cohort design. Example:

```yaml
# my_cohorts.yaml
- label: WT_13p2_microglia_homeostatic
  celltype: Microglia_Homeostatic
  suffixes: [9, 10]
  gex_clusters: [21]
  n: 250

- label: AD_5p7_microglia_earlyDAM
  celltype: Microglia_EarlyDAM
  suffixes: [7, 8]
  gex_clusters: [21, 22]
  n: 250
```

JSON works identically:

```json
[
  {
    "label": "WT_13p2_microglia_homeostatic",
    "celltype": "Microglia_Homeostatic",
    "suffixes": [9, 10],
    "gex_clusters": [21],
    "n": 250
  }
]
```

Each entry must contain: `label` (string), `celltype` (string),
`suffixes` (list of barcode suffix integers), `gex_clusters` (list of
official 10x GEX cluster IDs), `n` (target number of nuclei to
subsample).

## 9. Output schema

After `subsample_dataset.py` finishes, `data_demo/processed/` contains:

| File                                          | Size (approx) | Description                                                |
|-----------------------------------------------|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| `microglia_dam_demo.h5ad`                     | ~ 3 MB        | AnnData object (RNA + ATAC, obs/var with full metadata)   |
| `microglia_dam_demo.rds`                      | ~ 2 MB        | Seurat v4 object (via SeuratDisk::Convert)                 |
| `atac_fragments_chr7_chr15_chr17.tsv.gz`      | ~ 500 MB      | ATAC fragments restricted to chr7 + chr15 + chr17          |
| `atac_fragments_chr7_chr15_chr17.tsv.gz.tbi`  | ~ 1 MB        | Tabix index (only if `tabix` is on PATH)                   |
| `subsample_summary.json`                      | < 10 KB       | Machine-readable run summary                               |

The `obs` columns of the AnnData object are:

- `sample_suffix`     : int barcode suffix
- `sample`            : original sample name (e.g. `AD_5p7_rep2`)
- `CellType`          : `Microglia_Homeostatic` | `Microglia_EarlyDAM` | `Microglia_LateDAM`
- `gex_cluster_10x`   : official 10x GEX graphclust cluster ID (21 or 22)
- `MicrogliaScore`    : mean log1p of the 4 marker genes (QC, not gating)

The `var` columns are:

- `feature_types`     : `Gene Expression` or `Peaks` (from the 10x H5)
- `modality`          : `RNA` or `ATAC` (derived)

## 10. Limitations and future work

| Limitation                                                                  | Workaround                                                                                |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| AD_5p7 (Early DAM) has only 263 microglia cells total.                     | The script auto-downsizes the cohort if `n > available`. 200-cell default fits.            |
| 10x AppNote does not have a literal 13.2-month AD sample.                   | Use WT_13p2 (homeostatic) and AD_5p7 (early DAM) and AD_17p9 (late DAM) as a temporal axis.|
| `tabix` is not always installed on developer laptops.                       | Pure-Python fallback path in `subsample_dataset.py` (`--no-tabix`).                       |
| Ensembl 79 (`mm10`) is the reference for chromosome lengths in `chromosome_length`. The Signac/Cicero R pipeline uses `bioconductor-ensdb.mmusculus.v79` (now in `containers/environment.yml`). If you are on a custom `mm39` build, adjust the `--target-chroms` flag and add the appropriate EnsDb. | |
