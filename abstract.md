Beyond the Data: Workflow management and Gene Regulatory Networks in scMultiome
Daniel Mouzo1
1 Centre for Haemato-Oncology, Barts Cancer Institute, Queen Mary University of London, London, United Kingdom
In modern bioinformatics, generating high-throughput data is no longer the bottleneck; the real challenge lies in orchestrating its immense complexity with precision and efficiency. As single-cell becomes the standard for dissecting cellular heterogeneity, the demand for robust computational frameworks has never been higher. This hands-on workshop bridges the critical gap between scalable software engineering and biology.
We will first explore the principles of modern workflow management, demonstrating how to build highly reproducible, containerized multiomics pipelines using Nextflow. By decoupling pipeline logic from compute environments, we ensure that your analytical architecture scales effortlessly from a local workstation to high-performance computing clusters or the cloud.
Putting these engineering principles into biological practice, we will deploy these robust workflows to map the intricate molecular landscape of Single-Cell Multiome data. Specifically, we will leverage advanced computational tools like CellOracle to reconstruct high-resolution Gene Regulatory Networks by integrating ATAC and RNA data. Moving beyond static network descriptions, we will explore how to perform in silico transcription factor perturbations, simulating gene knockouts to model cell fate transitions dynamically.
By combining Nextflow and CellOracle, we will see how elegant pipeline architecture makes simulating cellular futures.
